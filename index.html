Summer Village of Larkspur Alberta

About Beautiful Larkspur

Alberta has full of beautiful small towns. Though large cities like Calgary, Edmonton, or Red Deer, are often considered culture and beauty capitals, this doesn't mean other locales can't be beacons of these qualities too. Located centrally in Alberta, Larkspur is a quiet, small summer village with a population of 53. This charming enclave is where beauty takes shape in the form of lakes, mountains, historic main streets, forests, and architectural splendour, among other picturesque qualities. It is beautifully sitting beside Long Island Lake. Due to the similarity of the landscape to the flower Larkspur, the town has adopted its name. The tiny village was governed by Mayor Jason Meliefste and supported by Westlock County.

Getting to Larkspur

Summertime vacation or a small day trip is always the best. Living in Canada, it is essential to cherish that short inter-season, aka summer, in this frigid country. We always look forward to taking a break from the city centre and getting lost in a place where you can be free and peaceful. Larkspur summer village is just around the corner for you! The driving distance from downtown Edmonton to Larkspur Village is only around 127 kilometres. You will need about 1.5 hours to travel by car. On the other hand, if you depart from Calgary, it will take about 3 hours to drive to the village. Unfortunately, there is no public transport available in this small town. But on the bright side, this is a sign of your peacefulness achieved! (Hint: No public transport, not too many people around!)

Leisure Activities

Wildlife Viewing

Larkspur is away from the urban city, gifted by the natural scenery and wildlife. During the summertime, when the blossom blooms and bee buzzes, you can see deer and caribou in the forest of Larkspur, as well as a rich collection of birdlife. Grosbeak being the most common bird you will see around the village, you can also find plenty of ducks chilling on the lake and pelican habitat by the lake. You can see some wild and colourful mushrooms around the woods as well. For a more adventurous trip with your little ones, with a bit of luck, you can also find some moose remains in the woods!

Lake Activities

Located by Long Island Lake, people at Larkspur Village have the privilege of enjoying various lake activities. Besides birdwatching, you can enjoy boating, kayaking, paddling, canoeing and floating or anything you can think of on Long Island Lake. With the quiet trait of the village, you should take the chance of regaining your inner peace and restoring your soul with these peaceful yet satisfying activities with your loved ones. Picnics are one of the most popular activities in Larkspur during the summertime. Grab your family and your favourite beer, and dip in the lake!

Motorcycle Trails

Other than lake activities, motorcycle or off-road trails are also available during summer and winter for more active and adventurous activities. There are some excellent trails around Westlock county, which sit nearby the Larkspur Village. These trails around the swamps and lake have a great staging area with a porta-potty and some fire pits. They range from easy to moderate levels to cater to different motorcyclistsâ€™ needs. You can play in the quad in both summer and winter.

Lake Fishing

Long Island Lake is one of the most significant locations for sport fishing for folks who enjoy fishing. It has excellent campground facilities, including a good boat launch and pristine and clear water. Furthermore, Its location is very close to significant communities, which makes fishing a good sport for highly recreational use. Long Island Lake supports northern pike and yellow perch for fishing and recreational purposes. For more information about Long Island Lake fishing regulations, please visit this website.

Larkspur Library

Despite the size of the population, people living in this tiny town are building a solid yet comprehensive neighbourhood to enrich the local's quality of life. Larkspur Village has its first library in the city. Managed by a volunteer librarian Koni Getschel, the small building or hut is by the hall and can notice quickly. The construction is a small wood house with a lovely painting from one of the residents. The Summer Village purchased this project to benefit their residents all summer long. The library also hosts events throughout the year. One of the most popular events is "Family Storytime." This event is a program for parents with young children. Songs, rhymes, and stories are shared. This event is also suitable for children of all ages and abilities to enjoy and learn together.
